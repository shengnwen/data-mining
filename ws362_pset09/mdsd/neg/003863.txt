Jack London's efforts at social novels, such as &quot;Iron Heel&quot; and &quot;Martin Eden&quot; were failures, because they are very cardboard and unconvincing. Jack London, the master story teller of the juvenile boys adventure novels, had a social conscience, but was not very good with expressing the social issues of the day, issues which usually belong in manifestos, not in literature. Jack London was no Dostoievsky, a true literary giant
