There are moments in you life that you know you will remember forever: the moment after a nuclear explosion; the assasination of John F. Kennedy; the sinking of the titanic.  None of these momnets compare with the horror of  the experience of reading Mr. Bassett's book, &quot;Real Life.&quot;  There  are no real people in this book.  No real emotions are explored.  The  premise upon which the book is written is flawed.  The science wildly  improbable.  If I was trapped on a desert island and this was the only book  that I had with me I would wish for the mercy of blindness to deliver me  from this evil
