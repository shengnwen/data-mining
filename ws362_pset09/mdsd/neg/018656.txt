I have vowed that I would never read another Anne Perry mystery again.  Then in a moment of weakness and in the absence of anything more appealing, I bought and read Half Moon Street.  And it was more of the same tired, worn-out explication of Victorian mores, discussed inthe same dreamy, wordy, uninteresting way that one expects from this author.  While billed as a mystery novel, the mystery part takes up no more than 10% of the book.  Next time I really will know better
