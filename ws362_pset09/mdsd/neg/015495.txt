Although intriguing in its premise, Atoms of Language is really about finding a small set of distinguishing characteristics for languages.  These characteristics are given the generic term of &quot;parameters&quot;.  The author describes these parameters in an excruciatingly slow process.  One of them, for example, is whether a tensed sentence requires a subject or not.  Far from developing a &quot;periodic chart of languages&quot;,it is more like playing a game of twenty questions.  If this book describes the latest development of linguistics, then the field has devolved into trivialities
