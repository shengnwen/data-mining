The story is unoriginal, convoluted, violent, and completely unbelievable. The plot is reminiscent of his previous book, Three. The characters are severely underdeveloped, and often act and respond in irregular and uncharacteristic ways, given their backgrounds. The dialogue is trite, inconsistent, and unrealistic. It is also a rather violent book, filled with graphic depictions of hangings, shootings, and a character being beaten with a frozen chicken. One scene involves a character being forced to chew on his recently severed finger. Dekker attempts to weave his unrealistic tale into a philosophic exploration of both good vs. evil and the true nature of beauty, but ultimately fails to deliver
