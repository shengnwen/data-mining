This book is very well-intentioned, but it gives the same skin care advice to everyone, which is way off. My 82 year old mother gave me the book along with the request to get her a product to "combat oiliness." Hello? She's not a teen with an acne problem-- but a grandmother with dry skin and wrinkles. All to say that the book sounds very authoritative-- but is not sufficiently customized to really be of benefit in addressing the many different kinds of skin problems that different people with different skin have
