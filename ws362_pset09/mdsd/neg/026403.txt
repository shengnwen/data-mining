In his writing, Wood never takes a breath, or any other pause for serious reflection.  He manifests no rational evaluation of competing thoughts on any page of the text; he merely spews a haphazard series of diatribes.  Wood provides no new insights, but merely repeats Fawn Brodie's outdated and questionable citations to the 19th-century equivalents of National Enquirer sources.
