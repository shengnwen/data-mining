First off, if you like sensationalist writing or wild claims, you will probably enjoy this book.  Likewise, if you are a new martial art student, it does give you some new ways to think in different situations.  That said, you should know I am a third degree brown belt myself (testing for my first dan or degree of black within a few months.)  Some of what he writes is disparingly obvious - for example, did you know an AXE can be used as a weapon?  Or a rifle can be used for a club once you run out of ammunition?  Other points he makes can be dangerous if you tried to rely on them in a real-world application.  For example, he writes how he deterred an attack by giving the aggressor an menacing stare.
