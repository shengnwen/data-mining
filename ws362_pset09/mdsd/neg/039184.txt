A slender plot is bloated by Perry's incessant and repetitive psychologizing.  An editor should have restricted to two (three at the absolute outside!) iterations of: Monk's frustration about his lost antecedents; the tensions between Monk and Runcorn; Hester's brave-but-vulnerable background; Callandra's brave-but-veiled feelings for Kristian; the flame-like fascination Elyssa exerts; Kristian's heroic idealism; the characterologic impossibility of Kristian killing an innocent bystander; etc, etc.  When the denouement finally arrives, it is unprepared, implausible and distasteful
