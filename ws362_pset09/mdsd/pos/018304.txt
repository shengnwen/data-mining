The book waxes strongly of nostalgia. The photos depict a largely vanished Americana. The scenes along the Route vary widely. From the rural sections of the California hinterland around Barstow to where it passes through Los Angeles. 

But one striking aspect is that portions are still relatively unchanged from how they were decades ago. Certainly, in the countryside, this might be of little surprise. But even in parts of the Route in LA. We see how the Route has been largely bypassed by freeways. Which has contributed to a dimunition in traffic along the Route. Ironically, this has helped preserve some of the buildings and neighbourhoods. As in South Pasadena. There is a slight twinge of architectural decay and somnolence
