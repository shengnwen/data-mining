
Forgotten Fire


	
	In Adam Bagdasarines "Forgotten Fire" a boy named Vahn is pressed through mental and physical hardships. He goes through everything from watching each of his family members die. Being molested, and just trying to survive. He meets many friends and looses new and old friends. Mr. Bagdasarine does an amazing job at showing the feelings and sorrows of Vahn. You feel like you actually are there. This book is a perfect example of why war is bad. The Turkish soldiers kill the Armerinians, they are so descriptive about this. This book shows us how wrong discrimination is wrong. It also teaches you how to never give up even against the worst odds. "Forgotten Fire" invites you on a wonderful and suspenseful adventure.
