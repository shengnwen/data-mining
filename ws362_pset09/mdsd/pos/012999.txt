This is a long-awaited book that officially relates and closes one of the great political mysteries of our time.  Mr. Woodward is an artful story-teller and that is particularly valuable here where the principal of the story seems unable to contribute much to the narrative.  The book seems a little perfunctory in that the author had to tell his side of this most compelling Watergate drama and the tension, where it exists, mostly surrounds whether Mark Felt will eventually be able to contribute any meaningful perspective and insights.  Sadly, there is precious little of that so Woodward is left to fill the void and he does so ably
