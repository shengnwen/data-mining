This book is the second one of the "Pendargast" trilogy, and it succeds in leaving us hanging and gasping for the last part. Yes, the moves in this deadly game of chess between the present day Sherlock Holmes and his nemesis, the Mycroft turned Moriarty, are suffocatingly attractive. But, it leads you to a place where you would be forced to wait o-o-n-n-e-e year to know what happens next
