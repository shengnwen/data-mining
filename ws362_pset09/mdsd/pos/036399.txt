Coetzee, perhaps more than any other author, manages to capture and convey the paranoia and schizophrenia of viewing Africa through European eyes. Among its many layers of symbolism and meaning, Waiting for the Barbarians parallels the experience of white South Africa fighting against and (eventually) succumbing to black South Africa, a simultaneous embrace and rejection, resisting the alien and still attracted to it. A highly recommended book, perhaps even Coetzee's best
