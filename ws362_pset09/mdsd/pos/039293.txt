Some of the reviewers have mentioned that Heinlein's prediction of personal robots didn't come true. Well, replace "robot" by "computer", imagine the hero as Steve Wozniak and the partner as, ahem, and you have a story that seems less dated now than it did in the 1970s when I read it as a teen. Poor Dan is so wrapped up in his tech work that he doesn't notice the real world of business politics, but (as this is SF) he has a chance to time-travel, set things right, and even get the cat. What more could an engineer hope for
