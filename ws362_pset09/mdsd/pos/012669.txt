An easy read, well-plotted, and often surprising, these four stories should please most readers with a taste for the ironic. The philandering, eccentric character of &quot;Uncle Oswald&quot; is a hedonistic delight,  and the two stories involving him are certainly the better half of this  small collection. It takes a masterful writer to make such an amoral  protagonist work, in any context. The other two tales, involving a  wife-swapping and a widow ready to try intimacy again, are less gripping,  and a bit anticlimactic with their payoffs
