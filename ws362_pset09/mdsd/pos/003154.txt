Robert Musil is one of the 20th century's greatest prose stylists, and one of its gigantic chroniclers.  I hate to have to compare him with others--in a sense that's a senseless enterprise--but because he is still relatively unknown I feel compelled to say he's the peer of Proust, Joyce, Kafka, et al.  This is his first novel and it's strange, strange indeed, but I loved it.  Have read it twice.
