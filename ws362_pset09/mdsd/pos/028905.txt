Too many people judge books based on an author's previous and best works, when they should be judged on on each book as its own entity.
If one does this than "the Broker" qualifies as an entertaining read, full of intrigue, great backdrop, likeable protagonist, and just a plain old fun read.
Will it pose moral questions after finished?  No, but it will give you pleasure for enjoying a story with an interesting plot, characters, and a satisfying ending, and that is what matters the most in the end
