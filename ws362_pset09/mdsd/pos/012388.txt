Pauley is the most competent English-language authority on fin-de-siecle Austrian politics, from the Habsburgs to National Socialism. His books are consistently well-written and thoroughly researched. He is not long-winded at all and uses no pompous academic or postmodern rhetoric. Moreover, in his work on the NSDAP as such, he is not in the least melodramatic and does not shy from pointing out the achievements of the Party. On the whole refreshingly objective, as a scholar should be
