The book gives coverage of recent civil wars throughout the world. Noteworthy because outright "traditional" wars seem less prevalent these days. Only to be replaced by lower level conflicts mostly confined within national boundaries. 

It is argued that the causes of a civil war can be manifold, and not necessarily reduced to an exclusive-or choice between unsatisfied grievances of an ethnic or regional group and the greed of a central government. Elements of both can often be found in the wars surveyed in the text. But one should also try to look for causes specific to each conflict
