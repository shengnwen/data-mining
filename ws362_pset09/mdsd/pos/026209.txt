as a physician and surgeon, I felt deeply moved by John Berger's words and Jean Mohr's pictures... despite the awesome advancements in both medicine &amp; surgery, the essence of physician-hood lies within those finely distilled moments between physician and patient.  The relationship between physician and patient has slowly eroded since Berger's extended essay.  Yet as I intermittently re-read the text and stare at the all-too-familiar scenes of suffering and personal anguish, I experience a sense of hope and become acutely aware of the necessity of remaining a staunch advocate for my patients
