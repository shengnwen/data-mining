Though mostly repurposed from previous publications, Eggers' stories are just as entertaining and heartfelt as the memoir/novel one-two punch that put him on the literary map some six years ago. Meet, in this collection, a more varied and (relatively) more mature Eggers who employs his trademark free-flowing style in new and unique ways. If that doesn't sell you, the hardcover edition (McSweeny's, 2004) is leather-bound and comes with one of those neat page-holding tassel thingies.
