What sophomore jinx?  If "Big Trouble," was good, and it was, this one passes it up. even funnier, and that's a reach.  There just aren't many funny novelists now. as funny as Jean Shepherd was, he did short pieces. sustaining humor through the length of a novel, well, I can't name one modern writer who can do it other than Barry. many of the characters are lovable--Fay, Wally, Arnie and Phil, and the slimy ones are, as in "Big Trouble," truly detestable human beings who you hope die a horrific death. Barry is dead-on accurate about the TV news coverage--I've watched far too much of it, in many states, and a good weather disaster slams most local TV stations into panic overdrive, which he makes laughable in this book.
