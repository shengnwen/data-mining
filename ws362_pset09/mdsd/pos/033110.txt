My husband's parents owned an older edition of this book and its cover has become tattered with use over the years.  I have looked at many and even purchased some other guides to North American Birds for my husband to use in our home.  When he saw this one on the shelf he was very excited because he considers it the most authoritative, extensive, and appropriately organized guide to North American bird classification and identification.  The pages are also thick enough that my children can use it without fear of ripping them or crumpling the corners.  This is the best we've seen
