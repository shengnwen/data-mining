Pranab Chatterjee wisely cautions us to remember that there are ways of expressing nationalism that differ from the Western models. With examples from literature and history the author helps us explore the  &quot;inner&quot; spiritual or cultural world of Bengalis in colonial  India, a world they tried to keep safe and distinct from the  &quot;outer&quot; world of British-imposed politics. The writing in places  is a bit vague, but the reading is worth the effort to remind us that  wisdom does not begin and end in the West
