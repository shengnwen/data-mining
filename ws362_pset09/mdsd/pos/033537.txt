Getting to know the retired British machinist Keith Stewart is easy and familiar--a modest, somewhat overly-fat hobbit-like man in his sixties.  His evolution, though, was compelling--  a person whose personal integrity  forces him to sail to the South Seas--with the resources of only sixty  pounds sterling, his reputation as writer for _The Miniature Mechanic_, and  his personal qualities of intelligence, and untapped courage. That little  engine he made and carried in his pocket helped, too: a four stroke 7-cc  running on petrol with a little carburetor, a tiny magneto and miniature  sparking plug. &quot;The whole lot mounted on a little baseplate was about  four inches long, two inches wide and two and a half inches high.&quot
