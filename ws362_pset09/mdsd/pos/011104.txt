In this collection of essays, Nancy Fraser makes equally strong political and scholarly contributions.  Arguing that &quot;postsocialism&quot;  leaves the left lacking in utopian vision, she begins by attempting to  formulate such a vision.  Rather than choosing to focus on the goal of  recognition or that of redistribution (put another way, on cultural or  social issues), she argues that any effective leftist politics must work  for both simultaneously.  Throughout the book she maintains this balance  between cultural and social concerns and strategies for change.  The result  is both politically inspiring and intellectually stimulating
